Biography

Postdoctoral fellow at Massachusetts General Hospital and Harvard Medical School

I am a physician, scientist and a computational biologist with a passion to understand immune responses against chronic antigens. My overall goal is to integrate novel computational and wet lab techniques to answer questions about heterogeneity in immune responses.

After completing medical school from Stanley Medical College in 2013, I pursued a master's in Virology from the National Institute of Virology in India. Following Masters, I pursue a Ph.D. from Canada, where I studied the various factors responsible for spillover of viruses from bats to humans. During my Ph.D. I started training on bioinformatic skills using R. I have worked on projects involving gene expression studies (bulk RNA seq, Nanostring assay and single cell sequencing), viral whole genome sequencing, clinical data analysis and machine learning technology.

I am also extremely passionate about sharing my knowledge on computational analysis. I have developed a tutorial section for this endeavor.
